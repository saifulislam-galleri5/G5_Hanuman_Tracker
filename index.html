Location & Compositing Pipeline

1. Foundations of Compositing

  • Combine 2D and 3D elements seamlessly.
  • Match color, lighting, perspective, and grain to live-action.
  • Software: Nuke, After Effects.

2. Slap Comps (WIP)

Fast, rough composites used for internal reviews to validate AI BGs and test timing. Step-by-Step:

  1. Import Plate: Basic color space setup (ACES/linear).
  2. AI Elements: Check alignment, shadows, grounding, and motion blur.
  3. Basic Layering: Over/under placement.

3. Final Compositing Steps

  • Depth & Atmosphere: Add depth haze, Z-defocus, fog volumes, light rays.
  • FX Integration: Smoke, fire, rain, magic glows.
  • Edge Work: Multi-pass keying, edge blending, spill suppression.
  • Global Look: Final grain pass, interactive lighting, EXR Delivery.

3D Character Modeling Guide

Base Mesh & Topology

  • Avoid overly dense topology (Keep it AI-Friendly).
  • Maintain clean edge loops around Eyes, Mouth, Nasolabial fold, Shoulders, Knees.
  • Face Rules: Circular loops around eyes, radial flow around mouth. No stretched polygons.

Hair & Cloth Creation

  • Hair: Add haircards for long hair to allow manual rigging/animation if AI fails. Avoid triangles.
  • Cloth: Avoid single mesh creation. Make separate parts for cloths to prevent distortion during animation (e.g., Dhoti must be separated at legs).

Sculpting & Texturing for Semi-Realism

  • Avoid hyper-real skin pores. Add subtle cheek fat pads, soft eyelid thickness.
  • Textures: Use subtle roughness. High-res iris texture. Clean alpha edges for hair cards.
